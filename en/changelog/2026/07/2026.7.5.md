## New Features

### Restrict channels when starting a conversation

You can now define **who can use each channel** when starting a new conversation with a customer. The company controls which numbers or support accounts appear for each user profile — reducing risk and organizing the communication flow.

#### What is it for?

- Show in the channel list **only those allowed** for the logged-in user's profile
- Restrict a channel to **everyone**, to **specific roles** (e.g. managers only), or to **specific users**
- Automatically hide **auxiliary channels** when starting a conversation — only the primary channel appears
- Prevent unauthorized users from starting a conversation on a restricted channel, even if they try to force access

#### How to configure?

1. Go to **Channels** and open the desired channel
2. In **Settings**, find **Who can start a conversation on this channel**
3. Choose one of the options:
   - **Everyone** — any organization member (default)
   - **By role** — select authorized roles (agent, manager, sales, etc.)
   - **Users** — select specific people
4. Save the settings

#### How it works day to day?

1. When starting a conversation from a contact (phone, WhatsApp, Instagram, etc.), the platform lists only channels that are compatible **and allowed** for that user
2. Owners and admins still see all primary channels (for operations and configuration)
3. If no channel is available for the profile, a clear message is shown

> Full guide: [Restrict channels when starting a conversation](/en/guide/channels/start-chat-visibility)

## Security

### Server-side validation when creating a conversation

In addition to hiding channels in the interface, the server **validates permission** when creating the conversation. Users without access get an error and the operation is blocked — even if someone tries to bypass the on-screen list.

---

## Benefits

- ✅ More control over who uses each support channel
- ✅ Less risk of sending via unauthorized numbers or accounts
- ✅ Cleaner list when starting a conversation (no auxiliary channels)
- ✅ Server-side protection, not only in the interface
- ✅ Existing channels stay open to everyone until you configure a restriction
